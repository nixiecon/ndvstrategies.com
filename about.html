A decade in operations. A hard reset. Then something built on my own terms.

The story

I spent a decade in corporate operations — the last chapter as operations manager at a satellite communications company that served both commercial and government clients, including contracts with the Department of Defense and the Department of Homeland Security (Customs and Border Protection). I held a Department of Defense Secret Clearance to work those contracts.

Dashboards, compliance, facility security — the kind of work where a broken handoff ripples through a quarter. I was good at it.

But after 38 years without knowing I was autistic, the accumulated cost caught up. I hit a wall — not from the job, but from a lifetime of running without the right support. I had to stop and rebuild from the ground up.

What came out of that was clarity about which parts of the work I actually wanted to keep: the parts where I get to fix the system. NDV Strategies is that — the work I'm built for, structured on my own terms.
